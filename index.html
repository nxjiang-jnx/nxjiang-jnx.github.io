Nanxiang Jiang

I'm currently a third-year undergraduate student in Computer Science at Beihang University, enrolled in the Top Talents Program in CS. I'm fortunate to be advised by Prof. Si Liu and Prof. Zhaoxin Fan.

My research interests focus on understanding and modeling the dynamic 3D world from images and videos, with the broader goal of enabling intelligent embodied agents to perceive, act, and adapt in the ever-changing physical world. Recently, I have been exploring 3D reconstruction, image and video generation, and Embodied AI toward this goal.

I was born into an artistic family in Nanjing, China. In my spare time, I enjoy playing the piano, origami, calligraphy, and studying symphonic conducting.

Simulation for Hot Electron Interchange Mode (2026)
Zikang Yang*, Nanxiang Jiang*, Wang Ge, Tianchun Zhou, Bo Li, M. E. Mauel
Paper / Code (Coming Soon)

Built MATLAB simulation framework for Hot Electron Interchange (HEI) instability in dipole-confined plasmas. Derived dimensionless kinetic–fluid coupled equations and implemented nonlinear simulations reproducing phase-space hole formation and frequency chirping.

Single-Image 3D Human Reconstruction with High-Fidelity Textures (2024)
Nanxiang Jiang*, Xin Wang*
Paper / Code

Developed a monocular 3D human reconstruction method that jointly recovers detailed geometry and coherent full-body textures from a single image. Uses part-wise geometry estimation and side-view decoupled transformers.
